## RWT — Utility Liquidity Token

**RWT (Real World Token)** is the protocol's utility token designed to solve liquidity fragmentation, aggregate yield, support Ownership Token projects, and form a powerful ecosystem of RWA-backed products.

<Note>
  Areal has two core tokens with distinct roles: **ARL** is the governance token that controls protocol decisions through futarchy, and **RWT** is the utility liquidity token that powers the economic layer.
</Note>

RWT works as an index-like aggregator with a built-in vault. The vault selectively acquires <Tooltip tip="Ownership Token — a tokenized representation of a specific real-world asset within the Areal protocol">Ownership Tokens</Tooltip> — not all of them, but those chosen by the community — forming a curated reserve of real-world assets. Dividend yield from these assets flows back into liquidity, driving RWT's value growth and sustaining the ecosystem.

The vault composition policy — which Ownership Tokens to acquire, in what proportions, and when — is **determined by the community** through <Tooltip tip="ARL — the governance token of the Areal protocol used for futarchy-based decision making">ARL</Tooltip> governance.

## Cash Stream Distribution

Every real-world asset generates income — rent, interest, revenue. In Areal, this yield is distributed through a **unified, community-governed model** tied directly to Ownership Tokens.

Each project that emits an <Tooltip tip="Ownership Token — represents a specific real-world asset and participates in cash stream distribution">Ownership Token</Tooltip> generates **cash streams** from its underlying real-world activity. These cash streams are distributed to OT holders according to rules set by the community through governance.

## Governance Through Futarchy

Areal replaces opaque, committee-driven governance with **futarchy** — a model where decisions are evaluated by expected economic outcomes, not by subjective voting.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Why not traditional DAOs" icon="thumbs-down">
    Most DAOs rely on token-weighted voting: more tokens = more influence. This leads to narrative-driven decisions, governance capture, and zero accountability for poor outcomes.

    Futarchy replaces opinions with **market signals** — participants are incentivized to be correct, not persuasive.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="How it works" icon="scale-balanced">
    1. **Goals are defined** — the community sets measurable objectives
    2. **Actions are evaluated by markets** — prediction markets aggregate expectations about which action best achieves the goal
    3. **Best signal wins** — the action with the strongest market signal is executed

    Every decision is tied to a verifiable outcome.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="What the DAO controls" icon="sliders">
    Through ARL governance, the community decides:

    - which assets are onboarded
    - how cash streams are distributed
    - treasury allocation strategies
    - liquidity mechanics and protocol fees
    - all core economic parameters

    No single team controls these decisions — they are defined collectively.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
